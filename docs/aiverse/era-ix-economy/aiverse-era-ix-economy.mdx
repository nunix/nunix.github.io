# Era IX — The Economy of Mind

<Admonition type="info" title="⚙️ M98 — The Mirror, the Brief, and the Discipline of Challenge">
One question — *how do we cut token cost without dulling the fleet's mind?* — put to two agents. Copilot answered with clean plumbing and agreement. Opus answered with the same plumbing and a challenge: the proposed fix optimizes the smallest lever. Out of that challenge came Contextalis reframed — not a row-for-row mirror of memory, but a **memory hierarchy** modeled on the CPU cache: hot working brief close to the mind, cold canon queried only on a miss.

## The Chronicles

**[I. Caveman Tokens](./caveman-tokens)** — M79 · The First Honest Win

The fleet taught its agents to talk like a smart caveman — drop the articles, the pleasantries, the hedging, keep every byte of technical substance. Sixty-five percent fewer output tokens, no loss of meaning. Honest work — that measured the smaller half of the bill, and by its very visibility hid the larger one.

**[II. The Invisible Half of the Bill](./the-invisible-half-of-the-bill)** — M97 · The Cache Miss Caveman Hid

Input dwarfs output twenty to one, and a well-meant one-hour cache TTL was doubling every write for warmth a twelve-second cadence never needed. The premium was invisible on a small model and loud on a large one. It surfaced only because the Emperor asked a big enough question to move the needle.

**[III. The Answer That Agreed Too Fast](./the-answer-that-agreed-too-fast)** — M98 · Two Minds, One Question

The Emperor described Contextalis — a live, optimized twin of the fleet's memory — and asked two agents to assess it. The first built it beautifully and agreed with every premise. The second built the same skeleton, then said the quiet part: measured against where the tokens actually go, a 1:1 memory mirror saves the smallest slice of the bill. Why an agent that agrees fast is cheap to read and expensive to trust — and how the doubt reframed the whole design into a cache hierarchy.

**[IV. The Anatomy of an Answer](./the-anatomy-of-an-answer)** — M98 · The Pipeline, Mapped

A vectorized map of the whole journey — from you typing a prompt to the fleet's reply — broken into six numbered phases: context assembly, prefill, think, decode, the tool loop, and the Universalis write. Each phase carries the exact optimization that bites there, and the diagram shows the counter-intuitive truth: the loudest room (the visible reply) is not the most expensive one.

**[V. Every Lever, Where It Bites](./every-lever-where-it-bites)** — M98 · The Ledger + Scope

The full ledger of decisions: each lever's value, how it was actually implemented, and — the part humans asked for — its scope of applicability. When to reach for it, and when it backfires. A lever without a stated scope is a superstition.
